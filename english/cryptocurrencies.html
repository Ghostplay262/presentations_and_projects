The Basics

What is Money — and Why Does It Need Reinventing?

Traditional money (fiat currency) relies on central authorities — governments and banks — to issue, verify, and control it.

🏛️

Central Bank

Prints money, sets interest rates, controls supply.

🏦

Commercial Banks

Hold deposits, process transfers, charge fees.

👤

You

Must trust every intermediary in the chain.

The problems this creates:

Censorship
Banks can freeze or block your account.
Inflation
Governments can print unlimited money, eroding value.
Exclusion
1.4 billion adults have no bank account.
Slow & costly
International transfers take days and cost fees.
Single point of failure
If the bank fails, your money is at risk.

💡 Cryptocurrencies aim to solve these problems with math and code instead of trust in institutions.

Definition

What is a Cryptocurrency?

A cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security and operates on a decentralised network — no single person, company, or government controls it.

🔐

Cryptography

Mathematical algorithms secure every transaction and prevent counterfeiting.

🌐

Decentralisation

Thousands of computers worldwide share the same ledger — no single point of control.

📖

Transparency

Every transaction is publicly recorded and permanently verifiable by anyone.

Each node holds a full copy of the ledger. No centre = no single point of failure.

What is a Blockchain?

A blockchain is a distributed ledger — a shared, append-only list of records (blocks) that are cryptographically linked together into a chain.

🔗 Why "chain"?

Each block contains the hash of the previous block. Changing any block would break every block after it — making tampering instantly detectable.

🌐 Why "distributed"?

Thousands of nodes each hold a full copy. To rewrite history you'd need to redo the work on more than 50% of all nodes simultaneously — practically impossible.

Cryptography

Cryptographic Hashing — The Glue of Blockchain

A hash function takes any input and produces a fixed-length fingerprint. The same input always gives the same output, but even a tiny change produces a completely different hash.

Input: "Hello"
SHA-256 → 185f8db32921bd46d35…
Input: "hello" (lowercase h)
SHA-256 → 2cf24dba5fb0a30e26e…

⚡ One character changed → completely different hash. This is called the avalanche effect.

Hash functions have three critical properties:

1

Deterministic

Same input → always same output. Enables verification.

2

One-way (Pre-image resistant)

You cannot reverse a hash to find the original input.

3

Collision resistant

Practically impossible to find two different inputs with the same hash.

Verification

How Transactions Are Verified

Every transaction goes through a multi-step process before it's permanently recorded on the blockchain.

1

🖊️ Broadcast

Alice signs a transaction with her private key (proving ownership) and broadcasts it to the network. The transaction includes: sender, receiver, amount, and digital signature.

2

🔍 Mempool

Nodes receive the transaction and place it in a memory pool (mempool) — a waiting room for unconfirmed transactions. Nodes verify the signature and check Alice has sufficient funds.

3

⛏️ Mining / Validation

Miners (or validators) bundle transactions into a block and compete to add it to the chain by solving a computational puzzle (Proof of Work) or staking coins (Proof of Stake).

4

✅ Confirmation

The winning block is added to the chain. Each subsequent block added on top is another confirmation. After 6 confirmations (~1 hour for Bitcoin), a transaction is considered irreversible.

Consensus

Consensus Mechanisms — How the Network Agrees

With no central authority, how do thousands of nodes agree on the "true" state of the ledger? Through consensus mechanisms.

⛏️

Proof of Work (PoW)

Miners compete to solve a computationally hard puzzle. The winner adds the block and earns a reward. Used by Bitcoin.

✔ Security
Extremely battle-tested and secure.
✘ Energy
Consumes enormous amounts of electricity.
✘ Speed
~10 min per block (Bitcoin).
🪙

Proof of Stake (PoS)

Validators lock up ("stake") coins as collateral. The protocol randomly selects a validator to propose the next block. Used by Ethereum.

✔ Efficient
~99.95% less energy than PoW.
✔ Speed
Faster block times (12 sec for Ethereum).
✘ Wealth bias
More stake = more influence.

🛡️ Both mechanisms make attacks economically irrational — attacking the network costs more than any potential gain.

Mining Deep Dive

How Cryptocurrency Mining Works

Mining is the process of adding new blocks to the blockchain by solving a Proof of Work puzzle.

1

Collect transactions

Miner picks pending transactions from the mempool and assembles a candidate block.

2

Find the Nonce

Miner repeatedly changes a number (nonce) and hashes the block header until the hash starts with enough leading zeros — the target difficulty.

3

Broadcast the solution

First miner to find a valid hash broadcasts the block. All other nodes verify it instantly.

4

Collect the reward

The winning miner receives the block reward (newly minted BTC) + all transaction fees in the block.

MINING SIMULATION
Block data + Nonce: 0 → Hash: a3f9c2… ✗
Block data + Nonce: 1 → Hash: 7b2e1a… ✗
Block data + Nonce: 2 → Hash: f1d8c4… ✗
… (billions of attempts) …
Block data + Nonce: 4,293,847,201
→ Hash: 00000000a3f9c2b1… ✔ VALID!

🎯 Difficulty adjustment: Bitcoin automatically adjusts the target every 2,016 blocks (~2 weeks) to keep block time at ~10 minutes, regardless of how much mining power joins or leaves.

💰 Block reward halving: Every 210,000 blocks (~4 years), the block reward is cut in half. Started at 50 BTC in 2009 → now 3.125 BTC (2024).

Application-Specific Integrated Circuits. Built solely for mining. Dominates Bitcoin today.

🏭 Mining Pools

Solo mining is like buying one lottery ticket. Mining pools combine the hash power of thousands of miners and share rewards proportionally — much more predictable income.

⚡ Energy Costs

Electricity is the biggest expense. Profitable miners seek cheap renewable energy. Bitcoin mining consumes ~120 TWh/year — comparable to some countries.

A crypto wallet doesn't store coins — it stores your private keys, which prove ownership of funds on the blockchain.

🔑

Private Key

Secret 256-bit number. Never share this. Whoever has it controls the funds.

📬

Public Key / Address

Derived from the private key. Share freely — it's like your bank account number.

🔐 Seed phrase — your master backup:

witch collapse practice feed shame open
despair creek road again ice least

12–24 random words that can restore your entire wallet. Write it on paper and store it safely offline.

Summary

Key Takeaways

💡

What is crypto?

Digital money secured by cryptography, running on decentralised networks — no banks, no borders, no censorship.

⛓️

Blockchain

A distributed, tamper-proof ledger of all transactions. Blocks are cryptographically linked — changing one breaks all.

🔍

Verification

Transactions are verified by the network via consensus (PoW or PoS) — no single authority needed.

⛏️

Mining

Miners solve computational puzzles to add blocks and earn rewards. Difficulty auto-adjusts to keep the network stable.

Wallets

Store private keys, not coins. Hardware wallets offer the best security. Always back up your seed phrase offline.

Stay safe

Never share private keys. Use cold storage for large amounts. "Not your keys, not your coins."
